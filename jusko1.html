EMERGING TRENDS AND ISSUES IN ICT

ICT in Education

ICT plays a vital role in education, especially during the pandemic. Learners can study remotely through online platforms that provide flexible learning opportunities.

Artificial Intelligence (AI) chatbots and virtual assistants support learners by helping in research, writing, editing, and personalized learning.

Nano learning focuses on short and targeted lessons such as videos, flashcards, infographics, and mobile applications, making learning time-efficient and cost-effective.

AR and VR technologies simulate real-world experiences like virtual field trips and laboratory activities. Gamification motivates learners using games, rewards, and levels.

Figure: ICT in Education

ICT in Health

Hospitals now use advanced technologies such as robot-assisted surgery guided by AI. These technologies allow precision, faster recovery, and improved patient care.

Wearable devices such as smart watches, glucose monitors, and blood pressure devices allow remote monitoring of patients.

Blockchain technology securely stores patient health records, while IoMT and big data analytics help analyze patient data for better diagnosis and treatment.

Figure: ICT in Health

ICT in Business

Businesses use AI and big data analytics to automate tasks and improve decision-making. Chatbots and customer experience personalization enhance customer satisfaction.

Hybrid and multi-cloud strategies provide flexible, scalable, and cost-efficient services for businesses and customers.

ICT in Transportation

Autonomous vehicles and advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS) improve mobility, especially for the elderly and persons with disabilities.

Mobility as a Service (MaaS) offers flexible transportation without relying on private car ownership. Big data analytics helps predict vehicle maintenance issues.

AR and VR driving simulators are used for training future drivers and improving road safety.

Advantages of Technology Trends

  • Enhanced customer experience and satisfaction
  • Improved communication with stakeholders
  • Increased productivity and automation
  • Real-time and data-driven decision-making
  • Cost-efficient services
  • Creation of new business opportunities

Disadvantages of Technology Trends

  • Job displacement
  • Cybersecurity and privacy concerns
  • Digital divide
  • Overdependence on technology
  • Distractions
  • Algorithm bias and fairness issues

Issues and Challenges of ICT

While technology brings many advantages, challenges such as cybersecurity risks, ethical concerns, digital inequality, and data privacy issues must be addressed to ensure responsible and inclusive ICT development.

Learning Cubes

Along with these developments, issues also need to be addressed. Emerging ICT trends in education, health, business, and transportation help make tasks easier. Data Breaches

Data breaches occur when sensitive or confidential information is stolen from a system. This includes personal data, corporate data, and medical records. The challenges associated with this ICT issue include the following.

  1. Cloud misconfiguration
  2. Third party attacks
  3. Cost and impact
  4. Reputational damage

Figure 11: Data breach

Security Risks

Effective security measures such as access controls, encryption, regular backups, staff training, and secure data management are important to reduce ICT security risks.

  1. Malware infections that disrupt systems and steal data
  2. Network vulnerabilities caused by weak security settings
  3. Denial of service attacks that overload systems
  4. Social engineering that tricks users into revealing sensitive information
